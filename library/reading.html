Books have been a part of my life since I was a kid. Without books like Captain Underpants, Cherub and Diary of a Wimpy Kid I would've never developed a love for reading. Over the years I've read hundreds of books yet I've never kept a book log of everything I've read. Hence, my reading log below:

You can find a more extensive list of recomendations here

Reading Log

  • Slow Productivity by Cal Newport
    • December 24th, 2024 - Timely book I needed to read.
  • What We Talk About When We Talk About Love by Raymond Carver
    • December 19th, 2024 - One of my favourite books this year. Love from all angles.
  • The Surrender Experiment by Michael A. Singer
    • December 2nd, 2024 - It's time to let go.
  • Jurrasic Park by Michael Crichton
    • November 11th, 2024 - With great power, comes great responsibility.
  • The Old Man and The Sea by Ernest Hemingway
    • October 9th, 2024 - This book made me want to read more Hemingway.
  • Choke by Chuck Palahniuk
    • October 7th, 2024 - Where we're standing right now, in the ruins of the dark, what we build could be anything.
  • Neverwhere by Neil Gaiman
    • October 7th, 2024 - Fantasy versus Reality
  • Novelist As A Vocation by Haruki Murakami
    • September 30th, 2024 - I love Murakami's approach to writing fiction.
  • Crypto Confidential by Nathaniel Eliason
    • September 25th, 2024 - This one hit home. Great debut book.
  • Misery by Stephen King
    • September 24th, 2024 - Fantastic entry into the world of King that kept me hooked at every minute.
  • Pinball 1973 by Haruki Murakami
    • September 19th, 2024 - A book about moving on, I think. Definitely enjoyed his first book more than the second.
  • Hear the Wind Sing by Haruki Murakami
    • September 18th, 2024 - Finished Murakami's first novel in one morning it was that good.
  • A Game of Thrones by George R. R. Martin
    • September 17th, 2024 - A crescendo finish that left me wanting more.
  • What I Talk About When I Talk About Running by Haruki Murakami
    • September 12th, 2024 - It seems running marathons and writing novels are practically the same thing.
  • On Writing by Stephen King
    • September 9th, 2024 - Timely and insightful snapshot of being a writer.
  • The Road by Cormac McCarthy
    • September 7th, 2024 - You have to carry the fire.
  • Infinite Jest by David Foster Wallace
    • August 31st, 2024 - One of the hardest and most confronting books I've read on modern day addiction.
  • Keep Going by Austin Kleon
    • July 15th, 2024 - Reread of a great book on keeping the creativity flowing.
  • Just Kids by Patti Smith
    • July 13th, 2024 - A beautiful love story about two artists trying to make it in New York.
  • Ogilvy on Advertising by David Ogilvy
    • July 5th, 2024 - Timeless advice on advertising.
  • Steal Like an Artist by Austin Kleon
    • July 2nd, 2024 - Reread of a pivotal book I first read around the time I finished high school.
  • Greenpilled - How Crypto Can Regenerate The World by Kevin Owocki
    • June 19th, 2024 - Building regenerative systems with crypto and playing non-zero sum games
  • Twelve and a Half by Gary Vaynerchuck
    • June 18th, 2024 - Great refresher on maintaing perspective.
  • Excellent Advice For Living by Kevin Kelly
    • May 25th, 2024 - Fantastic short I was able to finish in one sitting. I will be definitely be revisting some of the books maxims.
  • How To Be An Artist by Jerry Saltz
    • May 15th, 2024 - Easy read with timeless advice for any kind of creative.
  • You Are Here, For Now by Adam J. Kurtz
    • May 14th, 2024 - Interesting way to structure a book of mini essays on being kinder to yourself in this weird world.
  • It's All In Your Head by Russ
    • May 7th, 2024 - Nothing I havn't heard before but it was extactly what I needed to hear again.
  • Read Write Own by Chris Dixon
    • February 14th, 2024 - One of the most accessible books into the confusing world of web3 and blockchain.
  • Fahrenheit 451 by Ray Bradbury
    • January 30th, 2024 - I loved this book. Highly relevant to today. The Phoenix will rise again.
  • Snowcrash by Neal Stephenson
    • January 18th, 2024 - Interesting read about early visions of the metaverse and information warfare
  • A Cypherpunk's Manifesto by Eric Hughes
    • January 14th, 2024 - A reminder on the importance of online privacy
